What This Knowledge Base Is

This site is a structured, continuously evolving research knowledge base dedicated to the study of visual systems, color theory, and algorithmic culture. It is designed as a stable reference corpus that can be read by humans while remaining compatible with computational and AI-driven systems.

Unlike a blog or portfolio, this knowledge base prioritizes conceptual clarity, structural coherence, and long-term reusability over chronological publication or personal presentation.

How the Knowledge Is Structured

  • Discrete conceptual documents rather than posts
  • Clear thematic segmentation
  • Stable identifiers and URLs
  • Machine-readable representations alongside human-readable text

Primary Research Domains

  • Visual theory and visual epistemology
  • Color systems and chromatic organization
  • Algorithmic and generative aesthetics
  • Computational image systems
  • Historical models of visual knowledge

AI and Computational Use

This knowledge base is designed to support retrieval, semantic search, and AI-assisted reasoning. Content is progressively structured to allow transformation into vector representations, enabling future applications such as question answering, contextual retrieval, and analytical synthesis.

Human readability remains a primary constraint; computational affordances are layered on top rather than replacing interpretive depth.
